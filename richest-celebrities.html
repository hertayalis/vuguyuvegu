    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Born and raised in Glace Bay, Nova Scotia, James graduated from Acadia University in Wolfville, Nova Scotia.     In the 1990s, after a decade of honing his skills in Los Angeles and Toronto, James had his breakthrough when he created and starred in the animated series "Get a Life" and the variety program "The Ron James Show." Both shows were well-received and established James as a rising star in Canadian comedy.

James's comedy often tackles Canadian life, politics, and the complexities of the modern world, all delivered with his characteristic wit and energy.

    The Ron James Show

    In 2009, James launched "The Ron James Show," a weekly half-hour comedy show on CBC. The show, which ran for five seasons, featured James's stand-up routines, sketches, and satirical commentaries on Canadian life.
